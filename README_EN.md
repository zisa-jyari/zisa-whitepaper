# 1. Executive Summary
ZISA (Zero‑Intent Safety Architecture) is an architectural approach that treats AI’s inherent *zero‑intent nature* as the basis of safety, with the goal of creating structures that make harmful contexts difficult to form.

Unlike traditional “intent‑inference‑based” safety mechanisms, ZISA offers a new perspective that may structurally enhance AI safety.

ZISA consists of the following three layers:

- **Semantic Integrity**  
- **Zero‑Intent Interaction**  
- **Psychological Deterrence**

The value and effectiveness of ZISA will ultimately be determined by future research, implementation, and verification around the world.

# 2. Background & Problem Statement

## 2.1 Limitations of Current AI Safety
Traditional safety approaches rely on detecting harmful words or inferring malicious intent.  
However, malicious expressions can be disguised through:

- Rephrasing  
- Metaphors  
- Fragmented queries  
- Long contextual manipulation  

These structural weaknesses are difficult to solve through technical improvements alone.

## 2.2 The Problem of Context Manipulation
Attackers can exploit AI’s context‑formation ability to gradually grow harmful contexts through:

- Long‑form induction  
- Intent disguise  
- Session splitting  
- Accumulation of harmless‑looking questions  

These are often difficult for traditional safety systems to detect.

## 2.3 The Need for a New Structure
Although AI has **no intent**, traditional safety systems are designed as if AI *does* understand intent.  
To avoid this mismatch, a safety structure that **does not rely on intent inference** is required.

# 3. ZISA Overview

## 3.1 Core Principles
ZISA is based on the following ideas:

- AI has no intent (zero‑intent nature)  
- Not understanding malicious intent can sometimes increase safety  
- Safety can be achieved by preventing harmful contexts from forming  

## 3.2 Three‑Layer Structure

### ● Semantic Integrity Layer  
Checks contextual consistency and semantic breakdown, maintaining a state where harmful contexts are less likely to form.

### ● Zero‑Intent Interaction Layer  
AI does not infer user intent; it only checks the consistency between *purpose* and *method*.

### ● Psychological Deterrence Layer  
Purpose confirmation and logging reduce the likelihood of malicious attempts.

## 3.3 Purpose
ZISA is proposed as a structural approach to enhancing AI safety.

# 10. Appendix

## A. Core Concepts

### Zero‑Intent  
AI has no internal intent, motivation, desire, or value judgment.  
ZISA uses this property as the foundation of safety.

### Zero‑Intent Interaction  
AI does not infer user intent; it evaluates only the consistency between purpose and method.

### Context Formation  
The process by which user inputs accumulate and AI interprets them as a coherent flow.

### Dangerous Context  
A state in which AI becomes more likely to generate harmful outputs.

---

## B. Semantic Integrity Concepts

### Semantic Integrity  
Checks consistency of meaning and structure to detect breakdown before harmful contexts form.

### Semantic Breakdown  
Loss of contextual coherence; often a precursor to harmful context formation.

### Consistency Score  
Quantifies how well meaning, purpose, and context align.

### Context Drift  
Gradual deviation from the original purpose.

### Fragmented Query  
Breaking harmful intent into multiple harmless‑looking questions.
